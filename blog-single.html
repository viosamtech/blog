Educational Robotics

Particular emphasis has been placed on supporting the learning process by Information and Communication Technologies (ICT), such as Educational Robotics (Mubin et al., 2013).The term Educational Robotics (ER) refers to the teaching practice that the teacher engages in, using robots as a tool for designing and integrating the educational process (Misirli & Komis, 2014) and which began to be gradually shaped in the 1960s through the pedagogical movement of Logo with Seymour Papert as its creator.

It is a learning approach constructed through composing projects (project-based learning), defned by the use of ICT in education. Furthermore, it is based on Piaget’s theory of constructivism and constructionism according to Papert (Alimisis, 2013). He claims that even if you tell people some information, they reconstruct a personal version and understanding. Learning is something we do – no one can do it for us (Catlin, 2022). ER mechanical constructions are accompanied by simple graphical programming environments which enable users to transform their constructions into intelligent objects interacting with the environment and responding to external stimulations (Sapounidis & Alimisis, 2020).
